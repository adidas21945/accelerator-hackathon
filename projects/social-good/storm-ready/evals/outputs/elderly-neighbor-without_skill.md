**What your elderly neighbor should do before the weekend storm (Saturday – Sunday) hits Boston**

---

## 1️⃣ Prepare for a “Hurricane‑type” system

| Action | Why it matters | How to do it |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| **Secure all outdoor items** – furniture, grills, trash cans, potted plants, hanging décor. | Strong winds (up to 45 mph) can blow things over or cause them to become projectiles. | Tie‑down or store loose objects; bring in patio umbrellas, awnings, and any lightweight décor. |
| **Check the roof & gutters** – clear debris, seal leaks. | High wind + heavy rain will test your home’s ability to shed water. | Inspect shingles for missing granules; clean gutters/ downspouts so they’re free of leaves. |
| **Inspect windows and doors** – ensure storm‑shutter panels or plywood are ready. | Windows can burst under pressure, allowing wind‑borne debris inside. | Have pre‑cut sheets of ½‑inch marine plywood (or storm‑shutter hardware) on hand; drill any extra screws now. |
| **Battery‑powered flashlights & extra batteries** – keep them in a dry place. | Power outages are likely after the high wind warning expires. | Place one by each bed and near the bathroom/kitchen. |
| **Portable charger / power bank** for phone/landline. | Keeps you able to call 911 or receive alerts if the grid goes down. | Charge it fully before Saturday morning. |

---

## 2️⃣ Prepare for heavy rain & flooding

| Action | Why it matters | How to do it |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| **Elevate valuables** – important papers, electronics, medications. | Even a few inches of water can damage floors and cause short circuits. | Move items to shelves on the second floor or stackable containers; label “high‑value.” |
| **Create an emergency kit** (see below). | You’ll need it if you must evacuate quickly or stay in place for several days. | Keep everything in a sturdy, waterproof bag that can be carried easily. |
| **Know your evacuation routes** – primary roads and any low‑lying streets prone to flooding. | The coastal flood warning covers parts of Suffolk, Norfolk, Barnstable, etc., where water could rise quickly after heavy rain. | Identify at least two ways out (e.g., I‑95 northbound, Route 1/2). Keep a printed map in the kit. |
| **Check basement or lower‑level drainage** – ensure sump pump is functional and battery backup is charged. | Flooding can overwhelm natural drains; you may need to use your own pumps. | Test the pump now (run it for 5 min) and replace batteries if needed. |

---

## 3️⃣ Emergency‑Preparedness Checklist

| Category | Items (keep in a sealed, waterproof bag) |
|----------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Water & Food** | • Bottled water – at least 1 gal per person per day for 3 days.<br>• Non‑perishable meals/snacks (canned goods, granola bars).<br>• Manual can opener. |
| **Medications & Health** | • Prescription meds in original containers.<br>• First‑aid kit with bandages, antiseptic wipes, pain reliever, any needed inhalers or diabetic supplies.<br>• List of allergies & emergency contacts. |
| **Clothing & Bedding** | • Warm layers (blankets) for cooler night temps.<br>• Raincoat & waterproof shoes/boots.<br>• Extra socks & underwear. |
| **Communication** | • Fully charged cell phone + charger.<br>• Portable radio with NOAA‑weather alerts (battery powered).<br>• Flashlights & extra batteries.<br>• Written emergency contacts (family, neighbors, doctor). |
| **Tools & Supplies** | • Hammer, nails, duct tape for securing doors/windows.<br>• Ladder (if safe to use) – keep it in a dry spot.<br>• Multi‑tool or small wrench for plumbing. |
| **Important Documents** | • Copies of ID, insurance policies, lease/rental agreement, medical records.<br>• Store them in a waterproof container; consider scanning and saving digitally on a phone with offline access. |

---

## 4️⃣ Day‑of‑Storm Actions (Saturday – Sunday)

| Time | What to Do |
|------|------------|
| **Early Saturday morning** | Open the kit, review the checklist, confirm all windows/doors are secured. Check that the sump pump runs smoothly and battery backup is on. |
| **Before heavy rain begins (≈ Saturday night)** | Move valuables upstairs or to higher shelves; turn off utilities if you’re told to do so by authorities. Keep a flashlight handy for any power outage. |
| **If evacuation becomes necessary** | Follow the pre‑identified routes, stay on main roads away from low‑lying floodways. Bring the kit and keep pets nearby (if safe). |
| **During the worst of the storm (Saturday night → Sunday)** | Stay inside a sturdy interior room (basement or ground floor), avoid windows, and listen to NOAA radio for updates. Keep an eye on any flooding in your yard; if water rises quickly, move valuables upstairs immediately. |
| **After the storm** | Wait for official “all‑clear” before leaving the house. Check for downed power lines, gas leaks (sniff for odor), and structural damage. Document damages with photos for insurance purposes. |

## 5️⃣ Quick Reference: What to Expect Tonight & Saturday

- **Tonight:** Rain likely, winds up to ~30 mph, chance of precipitation >70 %. Temperatures will drop into the high‑60s.
- **Saturday (day):** Heavy rain and wind gusts up to 40 mph; storm warning in effect. Expect rapid flooding if you’re near low‑lying coastal areas.
- **Saturday Night:** Hurricane conditions possible – winds >45 mph, very high seas, heavy rain, and a strong chance of flash flooding.

---

### Bottom Line

1. **Secure everything** that can be blown or washed away.  
2. **Elevate valuables** and have an evacuation plan ready.  
3. **Assemble a compact emergency kit** (water, meds, flashlight, radio) and keep it in a waterproof bag.  
4. **Monitor the storm’s progress** via NOAA alerts; be prepared to act quickly if conditions worsen.

Stay safe, stay informed, and let your neighbor know you’re thinking of her before the worst hits!
