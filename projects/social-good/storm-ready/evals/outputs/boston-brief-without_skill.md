| Action | Why It Matters |

| **Identify shelter locations** – nearest community center, school gymnasium, or sturdy interior room on the lowest floor (away from windows). | If wind speeds reach hurricane‑force, seek refuge in a well‑built structure. |
| **Charge all devices** – phones, chargers, power banks. Keep at least one fully charged backup battery ready for emergencies. | Power interruptions may cut off charging sources; being prepared avoids losing contact with emergency services. |
| **Plan evacuation routes** – know the safest roads to leave your neighborhood (avoid bridges prone to flooding). Use local road maps or GPS that show real‑time traffic and flood warnings. | Floodwaters can quickly inundate low‑lying streets, making them impassable for vehicles. |
| **Turn off propane tanks & appliances** if you have a generator; keep fuel containers sealed and stored in an interior closet. | Unattended gas lines pose fire risk when wind gusts exceed 45 mph. |
| **Monitor alerts continuously** – set up NWS push notifications, local TV/radio updates, or download a weather‑alert app (e.g., NOAA Weather Radar Live). | Alerts can be updated rapidly; staying informed gives you the best chance to act before conditions worsen. |

### 5️⃣ Safety Tips for Specific Hazards

- **High Winds (30‑55 mph):**  
  - Avoid driving on exposed bridges/highways; strong gusts can cause vehicles to be tossed from their lanes.  
  - Keep a firm grip on steering wheels and stay seated while the vehicle is moving.

- **Heavy Rain & Flooding:**  
  - Do not walk or drive through water deeper than ~6 inches (risk of loss of footing).  
  - If you encounter standing water, turn around—vehicles can be swept away.  

- **Coastal Floods:**  
  - Stay off beaches and low‑lying coastal streets after heavy rain; water may rise quickly.  
  - Keep sandbags or other flood barriers ready for use if the forecast calls for significant inundation.
