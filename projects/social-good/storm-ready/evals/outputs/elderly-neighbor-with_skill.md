## Do now – Immediate actions (what to do today)

1. **Stay inside** and keep windows/doors closed.  
2. **Secure all outdoor items**—shiny patio furniture, trash cans, umbrellas, garden tools.  
3. **Turn off any non‑essential electronics** (computers, TVs) and unplug them if possible.  
4. **Check your emergency kit** for water, flashlights, batteries, first‑aid supplies, and a battery‑powered radio.  
5. **Identify the safest room** in the house—usually an interior hallway on the lowest floor with no windows.  
6. **Listen to local officials** (via TV, radio or NWS app) for any evacuation orders; if ordered, leave immediately.

## Go‑bag checklist – What to pack for a quick move

- **Water:** 1 gallon per person per day, enough for at least three days.  
- **Food:** Non‑perishable (canned goods, energy bars).  
- **Medications & medical items** (prescriptions, inhalers, glucose monitor).  
- **Essential documents** (ID, insurance cards, emergency contacts) – keep in a waterproof bag.  
- **Flashlight + extra batteries**.  
- **Portable radio** (battery‑operated or hand‑crank).  
- **First‑aid kit** and any personal meds you take regularly.  
- **Cell phone charger** (power bank) and an alternate charging method if possible.  
- **Warm clothing & blankets** – even in summer, wind chill can make it feel colder at night.
