**One‑Shot Hook:**  
You’re gathered in the dimly lit lobby of an old brownstone on Beacon Hill—its stone steps creak underfoot and the scent of damp wood hangs heavy. A thick fog rolls in from the Charles River, swirling around the wrought‑iron balcony railings that frame a cracked stained‑glass window. The building’s name is scrawled in faded copper lettering: **“Merriman & Co.”**  

A few weeks ago the place was a bustling antique shop; now it feels like an ancient spirit has settled back inside, whispering secrets and guarding something dark.

---

### Scene 1 – The Arrival (Check)

**Action:** You decide to push past the creaky front door.

### Scene 2 – The Hallway & Secret Room

**Description:**

A faint glow flickers from behind the far wall—just beyond the kitchen door you see a **glint of metal** reflected in the dust. The air feels heavy with something watching.

- **Search the room (Sully rolls 1d20+2).**

### Scene 3 – The “Secret” Room

The kitchen door opens to reveal a small, cluttered room filled with old jars of herbs, an ancient brass chandelier (still hanging but dim), and a single window that’s been boarded up.

In the center sits a **large oak table**, under which lies a **sealed wooden chest**. The air is thick with a low hum—almost like static electricity.

**What to Do?**

1. **Open the Chest (Sully rolls 1d20+2).**  
   - *If you succeed:* You pry open the lock and find inside an old map of Boston, some parchment notes, and a small vial labeled “Nightshade.” The room feels lighter.
   - *If you fail:* The chest stays locked; you hear a faint whisper saying, “Leave what you cannot take.”

2. **Search for Traps / Secrets (Murph rolls 1d20‑1).**  
   - *If you find nothing:* Proceed to explore the rest of the house.
   - *If you detect something:* You spot an old trapdoor in the floor near the staircase, covered with cobwebs.

### Scene 4 – The Attic & the “Merriman Curse”

The creaky ladder leads up into a dusty attic. Sunlight streams through a broken skylight, illuminating rows of antique books and a **large portrait** hanging on the wall—its eyes seem to follow you.

**Action:**  
- **Examine the Portrait (Murph rolls 1d20‑2).**  
   - *If you roll ≥ 8:* The portrait flickers, revealing a hidden panel behind it that opens to show an ancient sigil and a cryptic message: “Beneath the stone lies what was lost.”
   - *If you roll ≤ 7:* Nothing changes; the attic feels oppressive.

---

### Scene 5 – The Final Confrontation

**The Curse:**  
You discover that the house is bound by an ancient curse tied to a long‑dead ancestor who once owned the property. He died under mysterious circumstances, and his spirit still guards the treasure hidden beneath the brownstone’s foundation.

**Conflict Options**

1. **Attempt to Claim the Treasure (Sully rolls 1d20+3).**  
   - *If you succeed:* You manage to lift a stone slab from the basement floor, revealing a small alcove with the chest and a handful of glittering coins.
   - *If you fail:* The ground shakes; a spectral figure materializes—Merriman’s restless spirit. It lunges at you.

2. **Try to Appease the Spirit (Murph rolls 1d20‑3).**  
   - *If you succeed:* You place an offering—a small pouch of salt and a piece of bread beneath the portrait, which calms the ghost for a short while.
   - *If you fail:* The spirit grows enraged; it lashes out with spectral daggers.

---

### Climax – The Ghostly Showdown

- **Outcome:**  
  - **Ghost Wins** (if Merriman’s spirit overwhelms you): You take damage equal to half your total HP, and the house shakes violently. A new trapdoor opens in the wall, revealing a hidden cellar filled with cursed artifacts.
  - **You Win** (if you outwit or placate the spirit): The ghost retreats, leaving behind a small key that unlocks the main door.

---

### Possible Resolutions

1. **Take the Key:** You exit the house and head to the nearby tavern for some rest and maybe a quick sale of the recovered treasure.
2. **Return the Treasure:** You decide the cursed loot belongs back beneath the floorboards, sealing the room with a heavy stone slab (Sully rolls 1d20+4).
3. **Leave Empty‑Handed:** The adventure ends with you shaken but alive—perhaps to seek another lead in Boston’s underworld.
