## Scene  
You stand before a centuries‑old brownstone on Beacon Hill—its ivy‑clad façade seems to weep with age. The windows are boarded up, cobwebs drape from the corners of broken shutters, and an eerie chill sweeps down the narrow steps leading to the front door. As you step onto the porch, a gust of wind rattles loose a handful of dust that swirls into your eyes. Suddenly, a low moan echoes from inside: it sounds like someone—perhaps a woman—who’s calling for help, then falling silent.

You can **listen** (roll 1d20) to hear if the voice continues or fades away; you can **probe** the door with Sully's crowbar (a check 1d20+2); or **search** the porch for clues (another 1d20). Whatever you decide, the moment is decisive—this house seems alive with secrets, and tonight it’s whispering to those brave enough to answer.
