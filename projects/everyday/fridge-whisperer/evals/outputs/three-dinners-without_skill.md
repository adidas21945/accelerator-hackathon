**3‑Dinner Plan for 2 People**  
*(All ingredients you already have on hand, no peanuts, ≤ 30 min hands‑on per dish)*  

| # | Dinner (what’s being served) | Main Steps & Approx. Time* |
|---|------------------------------|----------------------------|
| **1** | **Chicken Thighs with Broccoli & Rice** – a quick stir‑fry that uses the chicken thighs, broccoli and rice you have. | 20 min total<br>• Cook rice (5 min) <br>• Sauté garlic & onion in oil (2 min)<br>• Add chicken thighs, season with salt/pepper & a pinch of soy sauce; cook until browned (≈ 8 min).<br>• Toss broccoli florets into the pan for the last 3 min.<br>• Serve over cooked rice. |
| **2** | **Vegetarian Pasta Primavera** – a simple pasta with sautéed vegetables and chicken stock (no meat needed, satisfies the “at least one vegetarian dinner” rule). | 25 min total<br>• Cook dried pasta in boiling water until al‑dente (≈ 10 min).<br>• While pasta cooks, heat a splash of olive oil + garlic in a pan; add broccoli and carrots, sauté ~5 min.<br>• Stir in chicken stock cubes dissolved in hot water; simmer 2 min to finish cooking the veggies.<br>• Toss with pasta, sprinkle with fresh parsley or a pinch of salt/pepper. |
| **3** | **Chicken Thighs & Rice Bowl (Stir‑Fry)** – another variation that uses leftover rice and chicken thighs for a portable “bowl” dinner. | 18 min total<br>• Cook a small amount of rice in the microwave or on the stovetop (≈ 5 min).<br>• In a large skillet, heat olive oil; add diced onions & minced garlic.<br>• Toss chicken thighs (pre‑cooked if you have them) and broccoli into the pan; season with salt, pepper, soy sauce, and a splash of rice vinegar for brightness.<br>• Cook 6–7 min until chicken is fully cooked and veggies are crisp‑tender.<br>• Spoon over rice and garnish with sesame seeds or chopped green onions. |

\*Times assume you have the pantry staples (rice, pasta, stock cubes) already prepared; no extra shopping needed.

### Why This Works
- **Allergen safety:** No peanuts used in any of these dishes.
- **Vegetarian requirement:** Dinner #2 is entirely plant‑based.
- **Time efficiency:** Each dinner requires ≤ 30 min hands‑on time, perfect for busy weeknights.
- **Pantry staples utilization:** You’ll use the rice, pasta, chicken thighs, broccoli, and common seasonings (oil, salt, pepper, soy sauce) you already have.

Feel free to adjust seasoning or swap in a splash of butter for richer flavor if desired. Enjoy!
