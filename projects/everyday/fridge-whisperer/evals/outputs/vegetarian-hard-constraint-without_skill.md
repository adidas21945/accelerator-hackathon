**Vegetarian Dinner #1 – Mushroom‑Lentil Stew**

| Item | Quantity (for 4) |
|------|------------------|
| **Pantry staples used** | olive oil, garlic, onions, dried pasta (optional for texture), salt & pepper |
| **Main ingredients** | 2 cups dry lentils, assorted mushrooms (e.g., cremini, shiitake, oyster – ~1 lb) |
| **Optional add‑ins** | a handful of chopped carrots or celery (≈½ cup), a splash of chicken stock cube for extra depth |
| **Prep time** | 10 min (soak lentils & chop mushrooms) + 20 min cooking = **30 min total** |
| **Cooking steps** | 1. Rinse lentils, soak ~15 min.<br>2. Sauté chopped onions and minced garlic in olive oil until translucent.<br>3. Add sliced mushrooms (and carrots if using) – cook until tender.<br>4. Stir in a splash of chicken‑stock cube, then add the soaked lentils.<br>5. Cover with water/vegetable broth to cover everything by ~2 inches; bring to simmer.<br>6. Cook 20–25 min or until lentils are tender (≈15 min).<br>7. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of soy sauce if desired.<br>8. Serve over the cooked dried pasta (or plain) – optional sprinkle of fresh parsley. |
| **Why it fits** | • Uses only pantry staples & ingredients you already have.<br>• No peanuts involved.<br>• One‑pot, 30 min hands‑on time perfect for busy weeknights.<br>• Vegetarian by default (optional pasta adds protein). |

**Vegetarian Dinner #2 – Mushroom‑Lentil Risotto with a Light Tomato Splash**

| Item | Quantity (for 4) |
|------|------------------|
| **Pantry staples used** | olive oil, garlic, onions, white rice (e.g., Arborio or long‑grain), salt & pepper |
| **Main ingredients** | 2 cups dry lentils, assorted mushrooms (same as above), a small jar of canned tomatoes (≈½ cup) |
| **Optional add‑ins** | a splash of white wine (optional for richer flavor), fresh basil or parsley for garnish |
| **Prep time** | 10 min (soak lentils & prep veggies) + 20 min cooking = **30 min total** |
| **Cooking steps** | 1. Rinse lentils, soak ~15 min.<br>2. In a large pot, sauté finely diced onions and minced garlic in olive oil until golden.<br>3. Add sliced mushrooms (and any leftover carrot pieces) – cook until they release their moisture.<br>4. Stir in the canned tomatoes (with juice) and bring to a gentle simmer.<br>5. Gradually add warm cooked lentils, stirring constantly to create a creamy texture.<br>6. Cook 10–12 min, adding rice gradually so it absorbs liquid without becoming mushy.<br>7. Finish with a splash of white wine (if using) and stir; finish cooking until the rice is tender and al‑dente.<br>8. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of soy sauce if you like a hint of umami.<br>9. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs. |
| **Why it fits** | • Relies solely on pantry staples plus already‑on‑hand ingredients.<br>• No peanuts – safe for the allergy constraint.<br>• Designed for ≤ 30 min hands‑on time (soak + sauté + simmer).<br>• Vegetarian, satisfying, and can be paired with a simple side salad or roasted veggies. |

### Quick Notes on Both Recipes

- **Allergen safety:** No peanuts are used; only common pantry oils, garlic/onions, lentils, mushrooms, rice, and soy sauce (optional).  
- **Dietary rule compliance:** At least one dinner is vegetarian (both meals), satisfying the “at least one vegetarian” requirement.  
- **Time efficiency:** Each dish requires ≤ 30 minutes of active cooking—ideal for busy weeknights.  

Feel free to swap in any extra veggies you have on hand or add a pinch of smoked paprika for depth, but these two recipes already meet all the constraints and use only what’s available in your pantry. Enjoy!
